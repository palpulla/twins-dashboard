## About Twins

Twins Garage Doors is a profitable, growing residential garage door repair and installation business based in Madison, Wisconsin. Founded by twin brothers Tal and Daniel. Tal is a chemical engineer (Yale BS) turned mechanical engineer (MIT MS and PhD). Daniel completed his BS at UC Davis and runs as CEO. Both founders are based in Cambridge, MA, and run the Madison business remotely.

We run on EOS (the Entrepreneurial Operating System). Vision, core values, quarterly rocks, and weekly L10s are documented and lived. We are opening a new location now and plan to add 1 to 3 technicians this year.

**Today:** 3 technicians, 1 CSR, 2 founders, and a fractional COO who is transitioning out. This role replaces her, and elevates the seat.

---

## Why now

We are building toward a sale to private equity in the next 2 to 3 years. To get there, the business has to be acquirable: measurable, predictable, well-documented, and not dependent on founder reflexes. That is the work.

---

## The role

You are the **Integrator**, in EOS terms. You run the day-to-day. You translate the founders' vision into systems, scoreboards, and execution. You are the person the team comes to with operational questions, and the person who closes the loop on accountability.

The role is undefined by design. We have not had it in this form before. You will shape it with us.

---

## The four priorities, in order

### 1. PE exit readiness
Codify SOPs across the business. Build the financial and operational reporting layer that a buyer's diligence team would expect. Document everything that today lives in the founders' heads. The goal is that a PE acquirer can see exactly how the business runs in a week, not a quarter.

### 2. Streamline operations
Find the wasted minutes in dispatch, the gaps in CRM data, the manual handoffs in scheduling and follow-up. Replace them with measurable systems. Use AI aggressively wherever it removes manual work. We currently pull data from HouseCall Pro, our CRM, payroll, and spreadsheets. The internal dashboard exists in early form and needs to be made real.

### 3. Team growth and accountability
Own hiring, onboarding, performance, and accountability for technicians, installers, dispatchers, and CSRs as we scale. Build the metrics and rhythms (scorecards, weekly check-ins, quarterly reviews) that make accountability easy and continuous. Hire 1 to 3 technicians this year, more next year.

### 4. Relationships forming
Be a presence in the Madison market and in any new markets we enter. Build the relationships with property managers, real estate agents, home builders, BNI groups, and adjacent service businesses that drive sustainable lead flow.

---

## What you will actually do, day to day

- **Hire and onboard** new technicians, installers, dispatchers, and CSRs as we grow.
- **Design and own the KPI scoreboard.** Repair vs install split, opportunity dedup, margin per job, dispatch efficiency, CSR conversion rate, tech utilization. Improve the source data feeding it.
- **Build SOPs** for dispatch, install, callback, complaint handling, vendor management, and hiring.
- **Run weekly L10s and quarterly rocks reviews** with the team. EOS rhythm.
- **Work directly with technicians.** Call them, problem-solve with them, ride along when needed. This is hands-on, not a desk job.
- **Use AI aggressively.** Automate dispatch communications. Build agent workflows that remove manual ops work. Prototype dashboards yourself.
- **Run the local relationships.** Property managers, agents, builders, BNI, community partnerships.
- **Report up to the founders weekly.** Numbers, blockers, rocks status. Tight, structured, no fluff.

## What success looks like

### By 90 days
- KPI scoreboard is real, weekly, and in the team's hands (not just yours)
- 5 to 10 SOPs documented for the highest-leverage workflows
- You have ridden along with each tech and met every dispatcher and CSR
- Weekly L10 and quarterly rock cadence is solid and predictable
- You have built relationships with at least 5 local partners

### By 12 months
- Hiring engine is repeatable. We onboard a new tech in 2 weeks with a documented playbook.
- The new location is operationally stable.
- Margin per job is up. Manual ops time is down. AI is a real lever, not a slide.
- A PE diligence team could evaluate the business without us scrambling.
- You are indispensable.

## Who you are

You do not need a specific job-title pedigree. The right person could come from operations at a small business, consulting, military, ops at a startup, or running their own venture. What you do need:

- **Initiative.** You see problems and fix them without asking permission. You bring more than you take.
- **Comfort with ambiguity.** "There is no SOP yet" is your home, not your obstacle.
- **Operating discipline.** You like measurable systems. You finish what you start. You return calls.
- **People skills.** You can build trust with a 50-year-old technician and an MIT-trained founder in the same hour.
- **Hands-on.** You will pick up the phone with a tech at 7 AM about a missed appointment.
- **Real AI fluency.** You have actually used AI to save time at work. You can name specific tools and specific outcomes.
- **Ambitious.** You see "build this for an exit" as exciting, not stressful.

It is a small business and a serious one. You will work directly with two founders who think like engineers (one with an MIT PhD). You will have real ownership and real consequences. The pace is fast, the timeline to exit is real, and the upside for the right person is meaningful.

If you want a defined role at a polished company, this is not it. If you want a seat that is yours to shape and a 2 to 3 year arc that ends in something material, talk to us.
