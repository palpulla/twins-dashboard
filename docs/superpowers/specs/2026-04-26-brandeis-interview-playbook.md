**Date:** 2026-04-26 (for interviews on Mon 2026-04-27 and Wed 2026-04-29)
**Format:** 30-min screening calls
**Goal:** filter in/out + leave the right candidates excited

---

## 30-Minute Flow (timer-style)

| Time | Phase | What you do |
|------|-------|-------------|
| 0:00–2:00 | **Open** | Warm intro + "Here's how I want to use the time: I'll give you a 5-min picture of Twins and the role, then I want to spend most of the call learning about you, and we'll leave 5 min at the end for your questions and logistics." |
| 2:00–7:00 | **Twins + role pitch** | Use script in §2 below. Don't read it word-for-word — internalize it. |
| 7:00–22:00 | **Their turn** | Ask 5 universal questions (§3) + 2-3 candidate-specific probes (§5). Listen. Don't interrupt. |
| 22:00–26:00 | **Comp + logistics** | Use script in §4. |
| 26:00–30:00 | **Their Qs + close** | "What questions do you have for me?" Then: "Here's what happens next: we're talking to a few people this week. I'll be back to you by Friday with next steps." |

**Hard rule:** at 28:00, start wrapping. Don't run over. Senior candidates respect time discipline.

## 3. Universal questions (5 + 1 bonus)

Pick all 5. Add #6 only if the candidate is moving fast and you have time.

### Q1 — Initiative & scrappiness
> "Tell me about a time you saw something broken in an organization that wasn't your job to fix, and you fixed it anyway. What did you do, and what happened?"

- **Testing for:** bias for action, comfort outside their lane
- **Green flag:** specific story, took real ownership, measurable outcome
- **Red flag:** vague generalities, "I'm always solving problems," frames it as a complaint about others

### Q2 — Operational thinking
> "Walk me through how you'd approach building a KPI dashboard for a small business with messy data across four platforms — say a CRM, a scheduling tool, accounting software, and a spreadsheet."

- **Testing for:** systems thinking, prioritization
- **Green flag:** starts with business questions ("what decisions are we trying to make?"), then data, then tools
- **Red flag:** jumps straight to tools (Tableau, Power BI, Looker) without asking what's being measured or why

### Q3 — People & accountability
> "Tell me about a time you needed a teammate or someone reporting to you to do something differently, and they pushed back. How did you handle it?"

- **Testing for:** ability to influence and hold a line
- **Green flag:** clear, respectful, named the change, named the why, real resolution
- **Red flag:** avoidance, escalation to manager, blames the other person, no real outcome

### Q4 — Comfort with ambiguity
> "If we hired you and dropped you into Twins on day one with no defined process, where would you start in week one?"

- **Testing for:** structured thinking when nothing is structured
- **Green flag:** observes first, talks to people, picks 1-2 high-leverage problems, ships something small fast
- **Red flag:** demands clarity before acting, tries to boil the ocean, only describes what they'd "learn" with no action

### Q5 — AI / automation in practice
> "Where have you actually used AI in your work, and what did it actually save you?"

- **Testing for:** real (not theoretical) AI fluency
- **Green flag:** specific tool, specific use case, specific time saved, can also name where AI fell short
- **Red flag:** "I use ChatGPT for everything," no specifics, oversells

### Q6 — Bonus, if time
> "What does success look like for you in your first 90 days here? What about year one?"

- **Green flag:** outcomes-oriented, modest in week 1, ambitious by year-end
- **Red flag:** title/comp focused, unrealistic timelines, generic ("learn the business")

## 8. Tells cheat sheet (general)

**Green flags (move to yes):**
- Asks about *the business* before asking about comp / title
- Names a specific failure of theirs and what they learned
- "I don't know, but here's how I'd find out"
- Pushes back on something you said with substance
- Gets specific fast (numbers, dates, names) without prompting

**Red flags (move to no):**
- Asks about comp in the first 5 min
- Buzzwords without substance ("synergy," "scaling," "transformation")
- Talks about themselves only in superlatives — never names a real flaw
- Says "I'd love to learn more" instead of forming an opinion
- Talks ill about previous employers / colleagues unprompted
- Doesn't have any questions for you at the end
