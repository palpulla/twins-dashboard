# Interview Script — Aman, Paru, Richa (Mon 4/27)

A single script. Sections 1–5 are the same for everyone. Jump to the right candidate's probes in section 7. Sections 8 and 9 are the same again.

---

## 1. Open (0:00–1:00)

> "Hi [Name], thanks for making time. I'm Daniel, one half of Twins. Quick on how I want to use the time: I'll spend about 7 minutes telling you about the company, our team, how we run our weeks, and the role. Then I want to spend most of the time learning about you. We'll leave the last few minutes for your questions and your timeline. Sound good?"

---

## 2. The company (1:00–2:30)

- Twins Garage Doors. Residential garage door repair and installation business based in Madison, Wisconsin.
- Twin brothers founded it. My brother Tal is a chemical engineer (Yale BS) turned mechanical engineer (MIT, both MS and PhD). I went to UC Davis. We both live in Cambridge, MA, and run the Madison business remotely.
- Profitable. Growing. Opening a new location now.
- We are building toward a sale to private equity in the next two to three years. That timeline drives almost everything we do.

---

## 3. The team and how we run (2:30–5:00)

**Inside the company:**
- **3 technicians** in the field doing the install and repair work
- **1 CSR** handling all incoming calls and scheduling
- **A fractional COO** who is transitioning out (this is the seat we're filling)
- **Me, the CEO** running vision, sales, finance, and founder-level work

**Outside partners:**
- Marketing agency
- Bookkeeper
- Outsourced HR
- CPA

**We run on EOS** (Entrepreneurial Operating System). Vision, core values, quarterly rocks, scorecards. All documented and lived weekly.

**A typical week:**
- **Daily MOJO call** every morning. The whole team. We review the previous day's numbers, talk through today's schedule, surface blockers. 10-15 minutes. Sets the day.
- **Techs head to their assigned jobs.** The CSR has prepped the schedule the day before.
- **CSR books incoming calls** throughout the day. Inbound is the heart of how we acquire revenue.
- **Once a week, full company L10 meeting** (EOS-style). Scorecards. To-dos. Issues. We review what hit, what missed, what's stuck.
- **Once a week, sales training.** We invest in skills. CSR conversion, in-home sales, technique.

The operating cadence is real. Not theater.

---

## 4. The transition (5:00–6:00)

Our fractional COO is rolling off. Rather than replace her like-for-like, we're using the moment to elevate the seat. Instead of a part-time COO, we want a full-time Integrator.

In EOS terms, that's the person who runs day-to-day, owns accountability, makes the team hit the numbers, and lets us as Visionaries focus on where the business is going. That is what this role is.

---

## 5. The role and 90-day success (6:00–8:00)

**Four priorities, in order:**
1. **PE exit readiness.** Codify the business so a buyer can read it in a week, not a quarter.
2. **Streamline operations.** I have a KPI dashboard partially rolled out, I'm iterating on it. You partner where useful, but the bigger job is making sure the team operates against the numbers and the numbers improve.
3. **Team growth and accountability.** Hire, onboard, and hold the line. Add 1 to 3 techs this year.
4. **Relationships forming, remote-first.** You build the outreach engine remotely. LinkedIn, email, phone, video, branded sends. Equip our techs to be ambassadors at BNI and at the job. Quarterly Madison visits to close in person.

**By 90 days, success looks like:**
- The team is hitting KPIs or visibly trending up on the laggards
- Every tech, CSR, dispatcher knows their numbers and reviews them with you weekly
- Communication between the team and us is tight. No "what happened with that lead?" surprises.
- A remote-first partner outreach motion is running, with 1-2 partnerships already producing leads
- You have ridden along with every tech and met every CSR
- Weekly L10 and rocks cadence is solid

That's where I want us to be by August 1.

---

## 6. Switching to them (8:00)

> "Okay, that's me. Let's switch and have you take over. Tell me about your background and what's drawing you to a role like this."

Let them go for 3-5 minutes.

---

## 7. Per-candidate probes (8:00–22:00)

Pick 3-4 candidate-specific probes (below) plus 2-3 universal questions (section 8). Total ~14 minutes.

## 8. Universal questions (use 2-3 to fill 14 min, after specific probes)

> Pick whichever 2-3 the candidate has not already addressed naturally.

**Q1. Initiative.** *"Tell me about a time you saw something broken that wasn't your job to fix, and you fixed it anyway."*

**Q2. Operational thinking.** *"How would you approach building a KPI dashboard for a small business with messy data across 4 platforms?"* (Listen for: they ask business questions before naming tools.)

**Q3. People and accountability.** *"Tell me about a time you needed someone reporting to you to do something differently and they pushed back. How'd you handle it?"*

**Q4. Ambiguity.** *"If I dropped you into Twins on day one with no defined process, where would you start in week one?"*

**Q5. AI fluency.** *"Where have you actually used AI in your work, and what did it actually save you?"*

---

## 9. Comp and logistics (22:00–26:00)

> "Quick on logistics: it is a remote role, working directly with me and Tal. Full-time is the preference, but we are open to starting as a paid internship around your Brandeis schedule with a clear path to full-time. We'd treat the first 90 days as a structured test period for both sides. Compensation is flexible, depends on the structure and your experience. We can get into specifics on a follow-up call if there is mutual interest. Equity or phantom equity is on the table for the right full-time hire. Any questions on that?"

---

## 10. Their questions and timeline (26:00–30:00)

**First:**
> "What questions do you have for me?"

**Then (always ask, regardless):**
> "Last thing. Tell me about your timeline. When could you realistically start, what's your school schedule looking like, and what else do you have on your plate right now? Other roles you are considering, other commitments, anything I should know."

**Why this matters:** filters fast on actually-available vs. browsing. Also tells you how to structure the next round.

---

## 11. Wrap (29:00)

> "Here's what happens next. We're talking to a few people this week. I'll be back to you by Friday with next steps. If we move forward, the next step is a deeper conversation with both me and Tal. Thanks for the time."

Don't promise a yes. Don't promise a no. Just a date.

---

## After each call

Open the decision matrix in the playbook. Fill in 1-5 on each axis. Yes / Maybe / No verdict. One sentence why. Do this within 10 minutes of hanging up, before the next call starts.
